# Forage ID Chatbot — Product Overview

## What It Is
A conversational chatbot application that helps farmers, ranchers, and agricultural professionals instantly identify forage plants. Users describe a plant or upload a photo through a chat interface; the chatbot returns a species identification, nutritional profile, toxicity status, and feeding guidance for their livestock.

## Users
- **Primary**: Farmers and ranchers managing grazing land and pastures
- **Secondary**: Agronomists and extension agents supporting farm clients
- **Tertiary**: Veterinarians and livestock nutritionists advising on feed quality

## Core Problem
Pasture managers frequently encounter unfamiliar plants or need to verify what's growing in their fields. Misidentification can lead to livestock consuming toxic plants, suboptimal nutritional choices, and costly management mistakes. Calling a county extension office takes hours; field guides require expertise most farmers don't have.

## Solution
A chat-based identification tool that removes the friction: describe a plant (color, leaf shape, stem, smell, growth habit) or upload a photo and receive an instant identification including:
- Common and scientific name
- Nutritional value: protein, fiber, and moisture content
- Palatability rating for cattle, horses, sheep, and goats
- Toxicity warnings — always surfaced first, before any other output
- Seasonal notes: when to harvest, graze, or avoid
- Suggested follow-up questions when the description is ambiguous

## Speed Target
Under 30 seconds from user input to complete identification response.

## Forage Species Covered
Common North American forages: alfalfa, red and white clover, tall fescue, annual and perennial ryegrass, bermudagrass, orchardgrass, bromegrass, timothy, sorghum-sudangrass, and major toxic weed look-alikes.

## Livestock Covered
Cattle, horses, sheep, and goats.

## Brand & Tone
Practical, plain-spoken, trustworthy. The voice of a knowledgeable agronomist, not a tech product. Accessible to users who may not be tech-savvy. Safety-first posture in every response.

## Strategic Principles
1. **Speed first** — identify forages in under 30 seconds of dialogue
2. **Safety above all** — toxicity warnings always appear before nutritional data
3. **Actionable output** — every identification ends with a specific feeding recommendation
4. **Accessible language** — avoid botanical jargon; pair it with plain-English translation when necessary

## Verified Operation
**Forage Identification Completed** — a user submits a plant description or photo and receives a complete response: species name, nutritional profile, toxicity status, livestock suitability, and feeding guidance.

## ROI Assumptions (adjustable baselines for projections)
- Estimated time saved per identification vs. manual expert search: ~20 minutes
- Representative skilled farm labor rate: $25/hr
- Value per identification: 20 min × $25/hr = ~$8.33
