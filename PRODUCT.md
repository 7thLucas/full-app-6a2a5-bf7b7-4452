# ForageIQ — Product Brief

## What It Is
A conversational chatbot that helps farmers, ranchers, and agricultural professionals instantly identify forage plants in the field. Users describe a plant or upload a photo; the chatbot returns a species ID, nutritional profile, toxicity status, and livestock feeding guidance.

## Users
- Primary: Farmers and ranchers managing grazing land and pastures
- Secondary: Agronomists and extension agents supporting farm clients
- Tertiary: Veterinarians and livestock nutritionists

## Core Problem
Pasture managers encounter unfamiliar plants and need fast, reliable ID. Misidentification risks livestock consuming toxic plants and costly management mistakes. Traditional resources (extension offices, field guides) are slow and require expertise.

## Solution: Conversational Plant ID Chatbot
Users type a description (color, leaf shape, stem, smell, growth habit) OR upload a photo and receive:
1. Common and scientific plant name
2. Nutritional value: protein, fiber, and moisture content estimates
3. Palatability rating for cattle, horses, sheep, and goats
4. Toxicity warnings — ALWAYS displayed first, before any other output
5. Seasonal notes: when to harvest, graze, or avoid
6. Follow-up clarifying questions when the description is ambiguous

## Speed Target
Under 30 seconds from user input to complete identification response.

## Forage Species Covered
Common North American forages: alfalfa, red and white clover, tall fescue, annual and perennial ryegrass, bermudagrass, orchardgrass, bromegrass, timothy, sorghum-sudangrass, and major toxic weed look-alikes.

## Livestock Covered
Cattle, horses, sheep, goats.

## Brand & Tone
Practical, plain-spoken, trustworthy. Voice of a knowledgeable agronomist — not a tech product. Accessible to non-tech-savvy users. Safety-first posture in every response.

## Strategic Principles
1. Speed first — identify forages in under 30 seconds
2. Safety above all — toxicity warnings always appear before nutritional data
3. Actionable output — every ID ends with a specific feeding recommendation
4. Accessible language — avoid botanical jargon; use plain-English translations

## Key Flow
1. User opens app → sees clean chat interface with a welcoming prompt
2. User types description OR uploads a photo of the plant
3. AI chatbot analyzes and responds with structured identification card
4. User can ask follow-up questions in the same conversation thread
5. Conversation history is preserved within the session

## Anti-references
- No complex dashboards or admin panels for the initial version
- No user accounts or login required
- No botanical Latin jargon without plain-English explanation alongside it
